Ada Lovelace

The First Computer Programmer in History

Timeline of Ada Lovelace's Life:

  • 1815 – Born in London, daughter of the poet Lord Byron.
  • 1833 – Meets Charles Babbage, the inventor of the Analytical Engine.
  • 1842–43 – Translates an article on Babbage's machine and adds extensive notes — including the first algorithm intended for a computer.
  • 1843 – Describes the potential of computing beyond mathematics, foreseeing ideas like music and graphics generated by machines.
  • 1852 – Dies at the young age of 36, but her legacy continues to shape the world of computing.
“That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal, as time will show.”
– Ada Lovelace
